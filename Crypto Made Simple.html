What is Crypto?

Let's start with the basics

Think of crypto like digital money that lives on the internet
💰
Regular Money
You keep it in a bank. The bank controls it. You need the bank to send it to someone.
🪙
Crypto Money
You keep it in a digital wallet. You control it. You can send it directly to anyone.
💡
Simple Way to Think About It
Crypto is like having cash, but digital. No banks needed!

Popular cryptos you might have heard of:

  • Bitcoin (BTC) - The first and most famous one
  • Ethereum (ETH) - Can do more than just payments
  • Dogecoin (DOGE) - Started as a joke but became popular
⚙️

How Does Crypto Work?

The magic behind digital money

📚
The Blockchain
Think of it like a notebook that everyone can see. Every time someone sends crypto, it gets written down. No one can erase or change what's written.
👥
No Single Owner
Thousands of computers around the world keep copies of this notebook. This makes it super hard to hack or break.
🔐
Your Wallet
Your crypto wallet is like a mailbox. You have an address where people can send you crypto, and a key to open it and send crypto out.
It's like a super secure group chat where everyone can see the messages, but only you can send from your account
🤯

Why This Matters

This changes everything about money

🏦
No Bank Needed
You don't need to ask permission to send money. No waiting 3-5 business days. No fees for "international transfers."
🌍
Works Everywhere
Send money to Japan or your neighbor - same speed, same cost. The internet doesn't care about borders.
👀
Everything is Visible
All transactions are public. This makes it really hard for people to cheat or lie about money.
🔒
You Own It
Your crypto can't be frozen by a bank. No one can take it away if you keep your keys safe.
💡
Think About It
Imagine if you could send $20 to a friend in another country instantly, for less than a penny, without asking anyone's permission. That's what crypto makes possible!
It's like having digital cash that works everywhere on Earth
⛏️

Mining & Scarcity

Why crypto has value and how new coins are made

🏃‍♂️
What is Mining?
Think of it like a race. Thousands of computers compete to solve math puzzles. The winner gets to add new transactions to the blockchain and earns new Bitcoin as a reward.
🎯
Why Mine?
Miners keep the network safe and running. They verify that all transactions are real and haven't been faked. They get paid in crypto for doing this work.
💎
Limited Supply (Sometimes)
Bitcoin will only ever have 21 million coins. But some cryptos like Ethereum and Dogecoin can make new coins forever. Each crypto has different rules!
📈
Getting Harder
Every 4 years, mining rewards get cut in half. This makes new Bitcoin harder to get, which can make existing Bitcoin more valuable over time.
Think of Bitcoin like digital gold - hard to mine and limited in supply
⚡
Fun Fact
Mining uses lots of electricity! Some people worry about this, while others say it's worth it for a global money system that never shuts down.

Different types of supply:

  • Bitcoin: Hard limit of 21 million coins (like limited edition cards)
  • Ethereum: No hard limit, but new coins are made slowly
  • Dogecoin: Unlimited supply - new coins made every minute
  • Regular money: Governments can print more whenever they want
🎯
Why This Matters
Each crypto has different rules! Bitcoin's limited supply might make it more valuable over time. Dogecoin's unlimited supply keeps prices lower but makes it good for everyday spending. It's like different tools for different jobs!
🚀

How to Get Started

Your first steps into crypto

1
Choose an App
Start with a beginner-friendly app like Coinbase, Cash App, or Robinhood. These are like training wheels for crypto.
2
Verify Your Identity
Just like opening a bank account, you'll need to show ID. This is normal and required by law.
3
Start Small
Buy $10-20 worth of Bitcoin first. Think of it as paying for a lesson, not an investment.
4
Learn and Explore
Watch how the price moves. Read about different cryptos. Take your time to understand before buying more.
⚠️
Start Slow!
Only invest money you can afford to lose. Crypto prices go up and down a lot!
🛡️

How to Stay Safe

Protect yourself from scams and mistakes

🔒
Keep Your Keys Safe
Your wallet password/keys are like the keys to your car. If someone else gets them, they can take everything.
🎯
Stick to Known Apps
Use popular, well-known apps like Coinbase or Kraken. Avoid random apps or websites promising "easy money."
🚨
Watch for Scams
If someone promises you'll get rich quick, it's probably a scam. Real crypto investing is slow and boring.
🚫
Red Flags to Avoid
  • Anyone asking for your wallet password
  • Promises of guaranteed profits
  • Pressure to buy RIGHT NOW
  • Celebrity endorsements on social media
Remember: If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is!
🎯

Quick Tips for ADHD Brains

Make crypto work with your brain, not against it

📱
Set Price Alerts
Use app notifications instead of constantly checking prices. Your brain will thank you.
⏰
Schedule Check-ins
Pick one day a week to look at your crypto. Daily checking leads to emotional decisions.
📝
Write Down Your Plan
Before you buy, write down why you're buying and when you might sell. Stick to the plan.
🧘
Stay Calm
Prices will go up and down. This is normal. Don't make big decisions when you're emotional.
🎉
You've Got This!
Crypto might seem scary at first, but you can learn it step by step. Start small, stay safe, and take your time.
